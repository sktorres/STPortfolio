Problem

Design a new car tablet experience with an emphasis on information organization and manufacturer advertising that is easy for users to view new car pricing, reviews and ratings.

Solution

Focused on an interactive experience that would utilize the tablet's feature set. Autoshow offers kbb.com content and opportunities for auto manufacturers to feature their newest models. Autoshow was focused on providing a clear and intuitive couch-surfing experience for tablet users.

User Research

The first step was to create a survey and post to our recruitment firm. The questions focused on defining how respondents research new cars, what platforms they use, current products they use, and what their overall process was. The resulting data revealed opportunites to solve problems. Then we conducted some ethnographic studies to validate the quantitative data we gathered. I conducted interviews to refine the problem and begin to structure the solution.

Competitive Analysis

To better understand what our competitors tablet experiences was like, I began research and analysis on competitors like Edmunds, TrueCar, and Manufacturer websites. SWOT analysis and user flow mapping revealed gaps in current services and untapped opportunities for a new product with a focus on simplicity, visual organization, and voice affordances.

Personas

Analyzing the survey and interview data exposed two main classes of users. These classes were utilized to create two user personas, the Safety Minded Influencer, and Expert Negotiator.

Molly

After the accident, we decided to find something very safe with room for our family.

  • Age: 33
  • Occupation: Preschool Teacher
  • Location: Los Alamos, NM

Motivations

  • buying a car for a family, not herself
  • uses blogs and friends opinions heavily
  • spends time chatting with other parents, influencing and being influenced by them
  • mid-level researcher who shares research with her spouse
  • immediate need for a new car because an accident put her old car out of commission

Goals

  • find a safe car
  • find something within her family’s budget
  • find something fairly quickly
  • interact with someone that at best she can trust

Frustrations

  • Molly finds the negotiation process so stressful that she refuses to do it. She hopes it won’t stress Mitch out too much.
  • Molly is very glad that none of her kids were in the car when she was in her accident, and safety could not be a more prominent concern for her as she looks for a new car.

Brandon

I have a new favorite car every six months.

  • Age: 35
  • Occupation: Sales
  • Location: Los Alamos, NM

Motivations

  • reads about cars for fun
  • passionate about cars
  • heavy researcher, but information is collected over time as he learns more about cars.
  • knows all the stats about his car off the top of his head as well as several of the cars he likes to read about.
  • checks the value of his current car every time he needs to get a new repair to see if it seems reasonable to get rid of his old car
  • prides himself on being the go-to guy for people
  • He keeps his car in pristine condition

Goals

  • get his current (within budget) favorite car
  • get a car he’s proud to drive
  • get a great deal (will walk out in order to feel like he's getting the right price, but the car is still primary.)
  • reinforce his perception (ego) that he's knowledgeable about cars and the car buying process

Frustrations

  • He can't sleep the night before he goes to negotiate cars.

User Flows

While keeping the personas in mind, I began with sketching out multiple iterations of navigation for this tablet experience. Our senior management team chose a concept with a wheel navigation for usability testing. After evaluation, I distilled the process and mapped out the final flows.

Wireframe Sketches

Using the user flows, I started to sketch concepts so that we could test the concept of the wheel navigation.

Then, I built out a low-fidelity prototype in Sketch. I finalized the wireframes in Illustrator. The wireframes were used to create a clickable prototype in Invision to user test.

Paper Prototype Testing

Testing was conducted in person and remotely using Skype with a test script. Feedback indicated the organize and move features needed to be redesigned to have greater visual prominence. Also, CTA’s needed to be relocated for better findability.

04_Branding & Identity

Brand Story & Logo

Developing the concept for the brand began with brainstorming and mind mapping. After conducting preference tests on two options, I decided on the name Ideabase. The brand story is inspired by the six components utilized when managing and unifying communities. Ideabase seeks to be friendly, reliable, and intelligently helpful while increasing efficiency.

The Ideabase logo is built on three concepts: an abstraction of the letters i and b, a project container housing your thoughts and ideas, and the concept of connection.

Typography

The typography was chosen to support ideas of unification and collaboration.

Colors

The main identity palette used colors that supported concepts of reliability and simplicity. Calm blue was used as the primary color and Burgeon black and secondary color.

Conclusion

Autoshow was a project that taught me the importance of testing throughout this design process. Knowing that simplicity was a priority, user testing helped me to determine when the design had crossed from obviously simple to confusing. Testing allowed for quick iteration and discovery of unidentified problems which resulted in a far improved final product and inspired a more informed solution. The refinement of responsive elements within this project developed my ability to design a better, more cohesive structure between mobile and desktop. Moving forward, my priority is to add more functionality to the organization and tagging features. Users indicated this is of high importance and would add overall value to the product beyond the MVP.
